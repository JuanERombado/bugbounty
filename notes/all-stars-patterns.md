## What the public data suggests

- Elite hunters are rewarded for confirmed high and critical impact, not for noisy scanner output.
- The All Stars page repeatedly highlights critical conversion, highs/crits, protocol specialization, and audit competition performance.
- Public profile skill tags include areas like lending, bridges, staking, L1, L2, DAO, DEX, AMM, yield, and asset management.
- Immunefi's All Stars support page says public evidence can include bug submissions, audits, tools, publications, and incident response experience.

## Workflow lessons to borrow

- Start with impact wording before looking for bugs.
- Favor high-value state machines: staking, rewards, bridges, escrows, accounting, permissions, and cross-chain message paths.
- Reject weak ideas early when they do not map to paid impact.
- Read audits before PoC work because known issues are usually out of scope.
- Use test-suite gaps as a guide to what the project may not have modeled.
- Keep PoCs small, local, and decisive.

## What not to infer

- Public profiles do not reveal private methods.
- Earnings and report counts change over time.
- A skill tag does not prove how a researcher found a specific bug.
- This workbench should learn patterns, not copy or impersonate researchers.
